Android Field, Learning, Observation, and Bounty Client

SpeciedexApp

SpeciedexApp is the planned Android application for field observation, offline data collection, species learning, chain monitoring, public exploration, validation feedback, and Bitcoin-funded bounty participation.

It can operate as a companion to a user-owned SpeciedexCore node or through trusted SpeciedexWeb and SpeciedexNet gateways.

The application follows an offline-first design so field work remains useful when connectivity is unavailable or unreliable.

It permanently rejects Homo sapiens records and does not provide human tracking, biometric, genomic, or person-profiling functions.

Purpose

A Mobile Field and Learning Client

Field Collection

Create structured observations with time, location, environmental context, notes, measurements, media, methods, confidence, and offline evidence.

Public Learning

Read species profiles, taxonomic paths, maps, media, ecological relationships, conservation information, and educational guides.

Node Companion

Connect to a personal SpeciedexCore node over a trusted local or remote path for private synchronization and advanced submission control.

Gateway Client

Use trusted SpeciedexWeb or SpeciedexNet gateways when operating a local node is impractical.

Mobile Workflows

Application Modes

Connectivity

Offline-First Field Design

Local Queues

Observations, evidence, drafts, signatures, and validation notes remain safely queued until a trusted synchronization path is available.

Regional Packages

Users may download selected taxa, maps, languages, guides, bounties, and validation rules before entering low-connectivity areas.

Resumable Media

Large images, audio, video, and field packages upload in verified resumable chunks.

Conflict-Safe Sync

Server changes do not silently overwrite local work; conflicts remain explicit and reviewable.

Data Capture

Observation Package

Identity

Scientific or provisional identification, common name, taxonomic context, confidence, and alternative candidates.

Time and Place

Timestamp, coordinate uncertainty, region, elevation, depth, habitat, and privacy-sensitive location handling.

Evidence

Photographs, audio, video, measurements, notes, specimen or environmental references, and cryptographic hashes.

Method

Survey, camera trap, acoustic sensor, transect, opportunistic observation, specimen review, literature extraction, or approved method.

Context

Weather, season, behavior, abundance, life stage, host, substrate, ecological interaction, and field conditions.

Provenance

Device state, app version, schema version, author signature, edits, source imports, and submission history.

Trusted Contribution

On-Device Checks

The app checks required fields, formats, media integrity, impossible values, duplicate drafts, human exclusion, and selected taxonomic or geographic rules before submission.

Node Validation

A connected SpeciedexCore node performs the authoritative protocol, evidence, provenance, policy, chain, and consensus checks.

Review Feedback

Users receive structured explanations for acceptance, rejection, conflict, requested evidence, or expert review.

Safety

Permanent Human Exclusion and Field Safety

Permanent Human Exclusion

Homo sapiens is forbidden from being entered as a species blockchain, taxon record, observation target, specimen subject, bounty subject, or derived profile.

The exclusion is constitutional to the protocol and is not an administrative preference that an operator may disable.

Boundary Enforcement

Validation rejects direct human records, aliases, disguised taxonomic entries, human biometric datasets, human genomic profiles, and records whose practical purpose is the tracking or profiling of people.

Incidental references to human activity may appear only as environmental context, provenance, authorship, conservation pressure, or interaction metadata attached to a permitted non-human species record.

Sensitive Species

The app may blur, withhold, delay, or generalize coordinates for threatened, trafficked, nesting, breeding, or otherwise vulnerable species.

Responsible Collection

The application must not encourage trespass, harassment, illegal collection, habitat disturbance, unsafe travel, or contact with dangerous organisms.

Application Design

Android Architecture

APK Distribution

Signed Android packages can be distributed through documented official channels with reproducible release metadata and checksums.

Encrypted Local Storage

Drafts, credentials, signing material, cached records, and private settings use platform-backed encryption and least-privilege access.

Background Work

Synchronization, downloads, uploads, feeds, and alerts use visible, user-controlled Android background execution.

Permissions

Camera, microphone, location, storage, Bluetooth, and network permissions are requested only when required by an enabled feature.

Local Models

Optional identification or classification models may run locally and remain advisory rather than silently writing accepted species data.

Interoperability

The app exchanges documented packages with SpeciedexCore, SpeciedexWeb, and SpeciedexNet rather than depending on a proprietary hidden backend.
